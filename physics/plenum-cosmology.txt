### experimental-paradigm

The paper discusses a comprehensive experimental framework to validate the Relativistic Scalar-Vector Plenum (RSVP) theory against the standard ΛCDM model. The RSVP theory suggests that cosmic structure formation arises from the interaction of a scalar entropy field, vector baryon flow, and a Voronoi-Delaunay tessellation governed by relativistic dynamics. Unlike the ΛCDM model, it does not require dark matter halos, predicting distinct geometric and dynamic signatures.

The experimental approach integrates four main components: laboratory analogues, astronomical observations, numerical simulations, and quantum turbulence experiments. 

1. Laboratory Analogue (Quantum Dot Systems): The paper proposes using a two-dimensional electron gas (2DEG) in a gallium arsenide heterostructure to model cosmic dynamics. The scalar field Φ is represented by a disorder potential, and the vector field v by directed electron flow. The local entropy is derived from density fluctuations via shot-noise spectroscopy with power spectrum P(f) ∝ S, where S is entropy. Experimentally, gate electrodes are patterned to impose a Voronoi tessellation potential, and a thermal gradient is applied to drive entropy dynamics. The electron density at Delaunay vertices is measured via scanning tunneling microscopy to test RSVP's prediction ρb ∝ |∇·v|, where ρb is baryon density.

2. Astronomical Observations (Cosmic Shear and Void Geometry): The study utilizes data from the Euclid space telescope and the Large Synoptic Survey Telescope (LSST) to measure cosmic shear γobs. RSVP predicts unique signatures, such as:
   - Filament Shear Alignment: Shear aligns with baryon flow, maximized at θ ≈ 0 (flow-filament alignment), unlike the ΛCDM model's θ ≈ π/4. The correlation function is C(θ) ∝ cos(θ).
   - Void Ellipticity: Void shapes depend on entropy with ellipticity e = (a - b)/(a + b) ~ S^-1, contrasting the ΛCDM's e ≈ 0.05. Cross-correlation between weak lensing and 21cm hydrogen data tests ⟨eS⟩ = ∫ e(r)S(r) dV.

3. Numerical Simulations (Computational Cosmology): Modified cosmological codes like AREPO and GADGET-4 incorporate dynamic Voronoi-Delaunay tessellation and entropy diffusion. Initial conditions use Gaussian random fields for Φ, with power spectrum P(k) ~ kn, n ∈ [-2.5, -1.0]. Metrics include Betti number ratios b1/b2 (filament-to-void balance) and Wasserstein distance 𝑊1 between simulated and observed galaxy cluster distributions.

4. Quantum Turbulence (Superfluid Helium-3): This experiment uses superfluid helium-3 at millikelvin temperatures to model cosmic filaments via vortex lines. A vortex lattice is imprinted with a Voronoi tessellation, and rotation rate is modulated to drive dynamics. Vortex reconnection is tracked using NMR spectroscopy, testing RSVP's prediction of vortex accumulation.

The proposed experiments aim to discriminate RSVP from ΛCDM through key observables such as void ellipticity, filament shear alignment, and halo lensing profiles. Positive results could redefine our understanding of cosmic evolution, while negative outcomes would constrain theoretical models. Future directions include refining laboratory measurements, reanalyzing Planck CMB data for v-aligned B-mode polarization, proposing JWST void observations for e(S), and releasing the TARTAN-RSVP code for benchmarking against IllustrisTNG at high redshifts.
